Marcelo Bielsa is one of those managers who divides opinion until you actually watch his teams play and then the argument is mostly over. I wrote this piece for Sportskeeda during his time at Leeds, when the club was mid-climb back to the Premier League after sixteen years away.

What interested me wasn't just the results. It was the _method_, the obsessive preparation, the 3-3-3-1 structure, the way he turned a Championship side into one of the most exciting teams in England. The disciples he's produced Guardiola, Pochettino, Simeone, say more about his influence than any trophy cabinet could.

Leeds got promoted that season. Bielsa did what everyone said couldn't be done.
